# Topics in Sovereign Debt

**Institution:** University of Copenhagen  
**Instructor:** Simon Hinrichsen  
**Semester:** Spring 2026  

---

## 📖 Course Overview
This seminar explores the multifaceted nature of sovereign debt, bridging the gap between historical context, theoretical frameworks, and modern institutional realities. We will examine why countries borrow, how debt is structured and managed, the conditions that lead to sovereign default, and the complex process of debt restructuring.

## 📚 Core Literature
The seminar is primarily built around the comprehensive volume *Sovereign Debt: History, Theory, and Modern Institutions* (Abbas, Pienkowski, and Rogoff, 2020), supplemented by advanced macroeconomic texts for rigorous theoretical modeling.

### Textbook Chapters
* **Chapter 1:** Public Debt through the Ages (Eichengreen et al.)
* **Chapter 2:** Concepts, Definitions and Composition (Arslanalp et al.)
* **Chapter 3:** The Motive to Borrow (Fatás et al.)
* **Chapter 4:** Debt Sustainability (Debrun et al.)
* **Chapter 5:** Debt Management (Jonasson et al.)
* **Chapter 6:** Reducing Debt Short of Default (Best et al.)
* **Chapter 7:** Sovereign Default (Ams et al.)
* **Chapter 8:** The Restructuring Process (Buchheit et al.)
* **Chapter 9:** Challenges Ahead (Bredenkamp et al.)

### Supplementary Theoretical Literature
* **Open Economy Macroeconomics** (Uribe and Schmitt-Grohé) – *Chapter 13: Sovereign Default*
  * Covers empirical regularities, economic sanctions, reputation mechanisms, and the quantitative and theoretical modeling of sovereign debt.

## 📝 Exam and Assessment Details
The evaluation for this seminar consists of student presentations (held throughout May) and a final paper/exam due in early June. 

**Requirements for the Final Paper:**
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper must demonstrate full mathematical accuracy and detail. Ensure all mathematical derivations and theoretical models are fully explained and thoroughly detailed without simplification.
* **Empirical Framework:** The empirical analysis portion of the exam must utilize the **Dornbusch framework**. Ensure all models, exchange rate dynamics, and macroeconomic adjustments strictly adhere to this specific framework.

Students will rely on international databases for empirical research, ensuring comprehensive cross-country and temporal coverage. Key repositories include:

* **World Bank/IMF:** Quarterly Public Sector Debt (QPSD)
* **BIS:** Locational Banking Statistics (LBS)
* **Historical Data:** Abbas et al. (2019) researcher database
